About:

I want to start with almost a love letter to the game I've played more times than I can count. Cyberpunk 2077 is a video game developed by CD Projekt Red. An immersive world where mega-corporations control everything and wars between companies have put the United States into a wasteland besides from a few states. You play as "V" who has been away from Night City but returns after things didn't work out in Florida. After a mission goes wrong V now has to find a way to save his life while getting a better understanding of Night City and the people who call it home. Playing this game is like being transported into a completely different world. You move around the world as if you're living in the city. The characters feel real and have their own motivations for their actions and their goals. From the city to the outback area of the game, each section has its own culture that you can further explore in missions or just walk around listening to NPCs talk. I think the reason I love the game is the theme it tells without directly saying it. What it means to be human even in a world where people are suited with robotic implants and lose touch with their humanity. How individualism and materialism can stray people away from making deep relations with the people they meet. The decisions you make have drastic consequences for others whether you decide to help them or not.

  • Highlights

  • 2D Screen: Since humans see in 2D working on your enviroment from 3D to 2D is important. She imphasizes using layers, repeating elements, and the shapes of the screen.

  • Movement: Miriam explains how movement is defined by two elements, Sliency and Affordances determine what the camera and the player sees.
